# Creativity

## Notes


Creativity is the capacity to produce something that didn't exist before — whether an idea, a connection, or an object. This Map covers both what enables creative output and how new ideas are actually generated.

[Conditions for Creative Thinking](/notes/conditions-for-creative-thinking.md) examines the prerequisites for creativity: the neurological states, cognitive postures, temporal patience, and design choices (including what to remove rather than add) that create the space for creative work to emerge.

[Creative Ideation and Innovation](/notes/creative-ideation-and-innovation.md) covers the modes of creative production themselves — remixing existing ideas, generative ideation, genuine novelty, and the role of imitation as internalization. Nothing comes from nothing; creativity is always shaped by what came before it.
