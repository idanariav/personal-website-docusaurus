# Integrity

## Notes

### Claim

Integrity is a proactive form of [Acting in alignment with your values is authenticity](/notes/acting-in-alignment-with-your-values-is-authenticity.md). Having integrity means that we live in accordance with our [Core values guide authentic identity and moral action](/notes/core-values-guide-authentic-identity-and-moral-action.md).

### Why it matters

### Examples

### Supporters

It is usually a sign that we have (or close to having) a [Achieving internal harmony requires integrating all parts of yourself](/notes/achieving-internal-harmony-requires-integrating-all-parts-of-yourself.md), because a person with integrity is likely to know who they are, what their values are and what they want out of life.

### Opposers

However, too much integrity might lead to a lack of [Flexability](/notes/our-beliefs-must-adapt-as-context-and-goals-change.md), because we are so sure of what the right path is and how one should walk it, that we fail to see the righteousness in other views [Diversity of perspectives strengthens decision-making and understanding](/notes/diversity-of-perspectives-strengthens-decision-making-and-understanding.md), and therefore lack the ability to even change ourselves depending on the situation.
