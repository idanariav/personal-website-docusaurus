# Critical Thinking

## Notes

### Claim

In general, critical thinking means to examine something before believing it, to search for possible ways of looking at it, different perspectives, looking for advantages and disadvantages, and validating the information we have. It is opposed to [Conformation bias makes us accept confirming evidence without scrutiny](/notes/conformation-bias-makes-us-accept-confirming-evidence-without-scrutiny.md) where we immediately accept what we see if it supports our view.

### Explanation

Critical thinking can't be done mindlessly, it requires our [Focus is concentrated cognitive effort that maximizes performance on a single task](/notes/focus-is-concentrated-cognitive-effort-that-maximizes-performance-on-a-single-task.md) and an activation of our [System 2 enables deliberate analytical thinking for complex problems](/notes/system-2-enables-deliberate-analytical-thinking-for-complex-problems.md) thinking. It is to approach a problem [proactivly](/notes/taking-active-control-rather-than-accepting-circumstances-passively.md), to examine not only the information at hand, but also to see which information is missing [Focusing on easy metrics blinds us to what matters](/notes/focusing-on-easy-metrics-blinds-us-to-what-matters.md), which other viewpoints are available [Binary thinking reduces complexity to false dichotomies](/notes/binary-thinking-reduces-complexity-to-false-dichotomies.md), whether it is presented in a biased way [How we describe something shapes how it is perceived](/notes/how-we-describe-something-shapes-how-it-is-perceived.md), etc.

### Why it matters

Critical thinking can also lead to [Ideation generates ideas through exploratory thinking freed from constraints](/notes/ideation-generates-ideas-through-exploratory-thinking-freed-from-constraints.md). By refuting and analyzing the current situation, we might come up with new and better ways of doing or thinking about something.
