# Zoom out

## Notes

### Claim

To zoom out means to change our [Perspective](/notes/subjective-perception-filters-how-we-see-external-reality.md) and gain [Clarity is achieved through definition and presentation](/notes/clarity-is-achieved-through-definition-and-presentation.md), and often combines with [Separating actions from identity allows growth](/notes/separating-actions-from-identity-allows-growth.md).

### Explanation

It is to either think of the "big picture", or the "long game" [Short term bias](/notes/we-prefer-immediate-benefits-over-delayed-ones.md). To step outside of the current situation and view it as an observer, rather than as an experiencer [Some knowledge requires direct experience to fully understand](/notes/some-knowledge-requires-direct-experience-to-fully-understand.md). It also means to think more abstractly, and try to connect different points of information that on the surface look different [Systems thinking reveals mechanisms enabling effective change](/notes/systems-thinking-reveals-mechanisms-enabling-effective-change.md).

### Why it matters

Often, zooming out allows us to take a [Break](/notes/rest-enables-recovery-and-sustained-performance.md), to [slow down](/notes/slowing-down-prioritizes-quality-over-speed-and-enables-reflection.md) which is essential for our wellbeing. It helps us avoid, or revert cases of [Emotional hijacking is uncontrolled System 1 dominance over reasoning](/notes/emotional-hijacking-is-uncontrolled-system-1-dominance-over-reasoning.md), by stepping outside ourselves, trying to see things as they are [Seeing reality without subjective bias reduces distortion](/notes/seeing-reality-without-subjective-bias-reduces-distortion.md), and think about other people and not just ourselves [Preferring personal benefits blinds us to others' perspectives](/notes/preferring-personal-benefits-blinds-us-to-others-perspectives.md).
