# Scarcity

## Notes

### Claim

Scarcity is the idea that some things in life, if not all, are a sort of a [Zero sum](/notes/binary-thinking-reduces-complexity-to-false-dichotomies.md) game. That we can't "have it all", and we must prioritize between what's important and what's not.

### Explanation

It can be between people, for example two people who are competing for the same job, and it can be within people, for example choosing how to spend your evening.

### Why it matters

### Examples

### Supporters

Some say that scarcity is the source for [creativity](/notes/innovation-is-generating-something-genuinely-new-that-hasnt-existed-before.md) and [efficiency](/notes/optimization-finds-the-efficient-point-balancing-outputs-and-inputs.md), because if we had all the resources in the world, if we would have lived forever, than what's the point in trying to do something better? What's the rush? It is precisely because scarcity serves as a type of [limit](/notes/self-imposed-limits-increase-ability-to-act-and-creativity.md) that it gives urgency and meaning to our decisions.

Similarly, scarcity is also what provides meaning to our lives. We can only do so much, be so much, that our choices have weights, every decisions has to be considered vs the [Every choice costs the alternatives you gave up](/notes/every-choice-costs-the-alternatives-you-gave-up.md) of not making it, or choosing something else. Every moment is [fleeting](/notes/experiences-are-unique-because-they-are-fleeting-and-irreplaceable.md), and there's only so [little](/notes/memento-mori.md) time on this earth, so every moment is potentially imbued with meaning because time passed is lost forever. We only need to [choose](/notes/triage-prioritizes-limited-resources-by-allocating-attention-wisely.md) wisely. It pushes us towards [Taking active control rather than accepting circumstances passively](/notes/taking-active-control-rather-than-accepting-circumstances-passively.md) and [Intentionality is conscious presence that replaces automatic default behavior](/notes/intentionality-is-conscious-presence-that-replaces-automatic-default-behavior.md), and forces us to [say no](/notes/saying-no-protects-time-and-energy-for-what-matters-most.md) to things we might like, but are not worth it considering what we lose by choosing them.
