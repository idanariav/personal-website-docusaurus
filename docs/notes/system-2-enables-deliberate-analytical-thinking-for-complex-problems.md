# System 2

## Notes

### Claim

System 2, as opposed to [Automatic thinking processes drive fast emotional responses](/notes/automatic-thinking-processes-drive-fast-emotional-responses.md) represents our [rational thinking](/notes/examining-ideas-rigorously-before-accepting-them-as-true.md). This is a slow and costly process, however it is useful for [complex](/notes/complexity.md) situations that can't be handled through an [automated](/notes/heuristics-are-mental-shortcuts-enabling-fast-automatic-decisions.md) basic response.

### Explanation

This system enables all that we are familiar with as an "intelligible species". Our ability to avoid [Emotional hijacking is uncontrolled System 1 dominance over reasoning](/notes/emotional-hijacking-is-uncontrolled-system-1-dominance-over-reasoning.md), activating both of our [Executive Attention Network](/notes/executive-attention-network.md) and our [Default Mode Network](/notes/relaxation-activates-inward-thinking-for-creative-problem-solving.md) are dependent on the existence of system 2.
