# Agency

## Notes


### Claim

Agency is the ability of the individual to logically connect between desires and actions, and become the inner, causal factor of those actions. Meaning, **a person with agency usually feels [responsible](/notes/responsibility-is-binding-yourself-to-outcomes-and-people-you-value.md) for their actions. They are aware of the decisions they are making, and their actions are lining up with their desires** [Examine your desires not just your actions](/notes/examine-your-desires-not-just-your-actions.md).

### Explanation

**The main case where we don't implement our agency is when there is no filter, thought or a conscious decision about the conversion between desire and action** [Impulsivity is acting without conscious deliberation or filter](/notes/impulsivity-is-acting-without-conscious-deliberation-or-filter.md).

It is important to note that agency is matter of following the right [process](/notes/focusing-on-process-and-consistency-yields-better-results-than-obsessing-over-outcomes.md), not a matter of which choices you make. A good process is one that:
1. **Free of external influence** - By [Pausing](/notes/rest-enables-recovery-and-sustained-performance.md), taking a moment to [Zooming out gains perspective through mental distance](/notes/zooming-out-gains-perspective-through-mental-distance.md), and allowing ourselves to [reflect](/notes/introspection-develops-self-knowledge-through-internal-examination.md) on our thoughts and feelings, we can make sure that our choices are free of any [External influence constrains authentic identity and agency](/notes/external-influence-constrains-authentic-identity-and-agency.md).
2. **Aligns with values** - Our choices should reflect our [Core values guide authentic identity and moral action](/notes/core-values-guide-authentic-identity-and-moral-action.md)

### Why it matters

Remember that in the space **between the pressure, stimulus and triggers we get from the external world, and the action we do as a response, we are always free to choose**.

### Examples

A person who sees an ad and immediately goes and buys something without thinking is not considered as a person with agency. Similarly, a person that is being shouted on and immediately shouts back is also not considered as a person with agency.

### Opposers

Making decisions without following the right process can lead to a false sense of agency due to:
1. **Rationalization** - Without even meaning to, we can mistakenly justify our actions after having done them to avoid cognitive misalignment, while beforehand we might have chosen differently [We justify our conclusions after acting, not before](/notes/we-justify-our-conclusions-after-acting-not-before.md)
2. **Surrendering to pressure** - Making choices without internal deliberation can cause us to feel like our choices are our own, while in reality we end up being a puppet in others' hands [Peer Pressure](/notes/social-environment-shapes-behavior-and-identity-through-norms-and-conformity.md)
