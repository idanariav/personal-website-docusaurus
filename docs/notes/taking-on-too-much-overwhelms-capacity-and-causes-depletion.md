# Overloading

## Notes


### Claim

Overloading is we take too much on ourselves, or spending too much time/energy doing something than we should not or could not handle.

### Explanation

It is usually the result of the [Hustle culture equates busyness with progress and causes burnout](/notes/hustle-culture-equates-busyness-with-progress-and-causes-burnout.md), which sees busyness as a virtue and rest as a vice, so we take on every single opportunity without stopping.

### Why it matters

However, [Rest enables recovery and sustained performance](/notes/rest-enables-recovery-and-sustained-performance.md) is essential to our wellbeing and productivity. Taking more not only leads to [Stress can be adaptive or harmful depending on perception and context](/notes/stress-can-be-adaptive-or-harmful-depending-on-perception-and-context.md) and eventually [Burnout is chronic exhaustion from overwhelming demands](/notes/burnout-is-chronic-exhaustion-from-overwhelming-demands.md), it also has [Each additional unit provides progressively less value](/notes/each-additional-unit-provides-progressively-less-value.md), where we are less and less effective, to the point where it could be harmful to the overall productivity, to due [Task switching incurs cognitive costs that reduce productivity](/notes/task-switching-incurs-cognitive-costs-that-reduce-productivity.md).

### Examples

Sometimes being overloaded is not a choice, it is thrust upon us, like kids with parents that fill their schedules with lessons and activities to the brim. [Margins](/notes/reserves-build-capacity-to-absorb-unpredictability-and-disruption.md)
