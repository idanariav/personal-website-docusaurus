# Pre-Mortem

## Notes

### Claim

Pre-mortem is all about [predicting](/notes/prediction.md) results and potential points of error before the project begins.

### Explanation

As opposed to [Analyze both results and methods to improve](/notes/analyze-both-results-and-methods-to-improve.md) where we analyze the results of a project after it was finished, pre-mortem is about foresight. Since in most cases we avoid even believing in [Failure is a necessary part of learning and growth](/notes/failure-is-a-necessary-part-of-learning-and-growth.md) and act with [Overestimating abilities blinds us to genuine risks](/notes/overestimating-abilities-blinds-us-to-genuine-risks.md), we fail to acknowledge the reasons that might cause it.

### Why it matters

Therefore, by [reversing](/notes/flipping-assumptions-reveals-hidden-possibilities-and-better-solutions.md) our thinking and act as if we have already fail, we create a [Symbolic fresh starts break entrenchment and enable change](/notes/symbolic-fresh-starts-break-entrenchment-and-enable-change.md) and a [safe space](/notes/psychological-safety-enables-risk-taking-and-vulnerability-without-fear-of-retribution.md) for creativity and coming up with all possible challenges and [solutions](/notes/handle-problems-while-they-are-still-small.md) to them.

This process helps us reduce or avoid common [errors](/notes/we-underestimate-task-duration-through-overconfident-predictions.md) while planning, and gain sufficient [distance](/notes/zooming-out-gains-perspective-through-mental-distance.md) from the project so that we could see its potential pitfalls.

### Examples

For example, we can state as a fact "our project has failed. Why is that?", the use of [Open-ended questions enable deeper understanding and meaningful dialogue](/notes/open-ended-questions-enable-deeper-understanding-and-meaningful-dialogue.md) is beneficial here, since it gets us away from [Binary thinking reduces complexity to false dichotomies](/notes/binary-thinking-reduces-complexity-to-false-dichotomies.md).
