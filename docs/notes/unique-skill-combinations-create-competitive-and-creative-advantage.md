# Skill synthesis

## Notes

### Claim

Everyone is good at something. It is wrong to believe that you are only able to be good at [one](/notes/multidimensional-identity-collapses-into-single-fixed-characteristic.md) particular topic [expertise](/notes/mastery-requires-deliberate-practice-and-hard-work-not-innate-talent.md).

### Explanation

Most likely, there will always be someone better than you in any one topic, but if you [diversify](/notes/breath-beats-depth.md) your knowledge, you will be able to find the **combination** of skills that gives you your [Finding your unique strength creates sustainable advantage](/notes/finding-your-unique-strength-creates-sustainable-advantage.md) and makes you the best at something.

### Why it matters

Finding and cultivating your unique skills is not easy. It takes both [Self-awareness reveals mental and emotional states enabling intentional action](/notes/self-awareness-reveals-mental-and-emotional-states-enabling-intentional-action.md) to know what you are good at without being [over confident](/notes/overestimating-abilities-blinds-us-to-genuine-risks.md), while also [experimenting](/notes/experimentation-is-the-only-reliable-way-to-improve-and-discover-truth.md) a lot until you find your unique combination.
