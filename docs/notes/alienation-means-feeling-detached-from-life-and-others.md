# Alienation

## Notes


### Claim

Alienation is the opposite feeling of [Belonging](/notes/inclusion-means-proactively-welcoming-and-believing-in-others.md). We feel detached from our lives, from what we do, and even who we are.

### Explanation

Alienation is usually mentioned as something that rose as part of the [Industrial Revolution](/notes/industrial-revolution.md), creating a wedge between the workers and what they produce, bringing [Depression is chronic despair from helplessness and lost meaning](/notes/depression-is-chronic-despair-from-helplessness-and-lost-meaning.md) into their lives because they have lost that which gives them meaning. Instead of being [immersed](/notes/flow-is-deep-immersion-where-performance-peaks-and-self-awareness-disappears.md) in what they are doing, they can't connect with it at all.

However, alienation is much more than our job, it's about our lives in general. While [Attachment distorts how we see the things we cling to](/notes/attachment-distorts-how-we-see-the-things-we-cling-to.md) has it's drawbacks, it is still essential to our lives. Without being [connected](/notes/unity-is-the-felt-sense-of-connection-with-others-and-the-world.md) to anything, without being [dependent](/notes/dependency.md) on anything, we have nothing that provides meaning and joy in our lives.

### Why it matters

Alienation can quickly lead to [Indifference](/notes/indifference.md), as we lack the connection that makes us care.

We feel [lonely](/notes/loneliness-is-the-painful-state-of-feeling-disconnected-from-others.md) and [exhausted](/notes/burnout-is-chronic-exhaustion-from-overwhelming-demands.md) constantly, wondering why even bother.
