# Fixed Mindset

## Notes

### Claim

Fixed mindset is the idea that our mental or physical capabilities are fixed and are determined in birth [Nature vs nurture](/notes/nature-vs-nurture.md).

### Explanation

According to a fixed mindset, [Failure is a necessary part of learning and growth](/notes/failure-is-a-necessary-part-of-learning-and-growth.md) is something to avoid because it [signals](/notes/signaling-is-displaying-qualities-to-appear-worthy-or-successful.md) to others that you lack the skills needed for success, and that it is an indication that whatever we tried to do is not a good fit for us, and we should focus on what we already do best and what comes naturally for us.

Fixed mindset is a [Harmful actions reinforce themselves through self-perpetuating feedback loops](/notes/harmful-actions-reinforce-themselves-through-self-perpetuating-feedback-loops.md), because every failure causes us to avoid trying again, which limits our ability to improve, which further increases the chances of failure.

### Examples

### Supporters

To see ourselves as something fixed and unchanging is to embrace our [Objectivity is loss of agency to external control and instrumental treatment](/notes/objectivity-is-loss-of-agency-to-external-control-and-instrumental-treatment.md), to view ourselves as objects rather than people capable of change. It's to deny the [Complexity](/notes/complexity.md) of human life, our vast potential and our ability to grow and change. It is to deny our ability to [Transcendence is the act of continuously becoming a better self](/notes/transcendence-is-the-act-of-continuously-becoming-a-better-self.md) and overcome difficulties.

Usually we experience a fixed mindset when we tie our success to our [Ego](/notes/distinguishing-appearance-from-authentic-substance-reveals-hidden-conflicts.md), which means that when we fail it indicates our own incompetence, a stain on our identity that we can't remove. We view success as [binary](/notes/binary-thinking-reduces-complexity-to-false-dichotomies.md), either we are talented and will always succeed, or worthless and will always fail. We [align](/notes/actions-and-physical-states-unconsciously-shape-our-beliefs.md) between who we were, who we are, and who we could ever be, always have been the same, always will be the same.

### Opposers

This is the opposite view of [Capabilities are malleable through effort and learning](/notes/capabilities-are-malleable-through-effort-and-learning.md), because according to a fixed mindset, there is no point in trying to learn something new.
