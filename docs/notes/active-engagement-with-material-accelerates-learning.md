# Learning by Doing

## Notes

### Claim

While [Mastery requires deliberate practice and hard work, not innate talent](/notes/mastery-requires-deliberate-practice-and-hard-work-not-innate-talent.md) usually involves both theoretical knowledge and [hands on knowledge](/notes/some-knowledge-requires-direct-experience-to-fully-understand.md), we learn much more when we [engage](/notes/active-engagement-turns-information-into-knowledge.md) with the material.

### Explanation

Engaging means to switch from a passive mode of learning such as hearing a lecture or reading a book to a more [proactive](/notes/taking-active-control-rather-than-accepting-circumstances-passively.md) version such as creating or [teaching](/notes/explaining-ideas-simply-exposes-gaps-in-understanding.md).

### Why it Matters

There's no escaping the truth that [Practice beats perfection](/notes/practice-beats-perfection.md). The only way to improve is by doing, so we have to get rid of any false idols of [Perfectionism paralyzes action through unattainable standards](/notes/perfectionism-paralyzes-action-through-unattainable-standards.md), embrace our imperfect knowledge, and just start [practicing](/notes/deliberate-practice-is-the-best-way-to-develop-skills.md).

### Opposers

Most of us avoid learning by doing because it:
1. **Damages our ego** - switching from consuming to generating knowledge can have an awakening experience, one that shows us the gaps in our knowledge. [Ego](/notes/distinguishing-appearance-from-authentic-substance-reveals-hidden-conflicts.md)
2. **causes us to fail** - When we only consume content, we can be safe from [Failure is a necessary part of learning and growth](/notes/failure-is-a-necessary-part-of-learning-and-growth.md) because we never try, while being proactive forces us to try and ultimately fail, most likely on the [very first try](/notes/first-attempts-are-inherently-lower-quality-than-iterations.md)
3. **Hard** - listening to a podcast is easy, writing one is hard. Even when we know that [Learning should be hard](/notes/learning-should-be-hard.md), it is still a natural tendency to avoid hardships when possible.
