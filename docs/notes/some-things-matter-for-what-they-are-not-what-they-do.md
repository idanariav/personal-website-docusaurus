# Intrinsic

## Notes

### Claim

Intrinsic is when we appreciate something for what it is, not for the [Utility quantifies measurable benefits and guides rational decisions](/notes/utility-quantifies-measurable-benefits-and-guides-rational-decisions.md) it provides us. It is to see beyond the [form](/notes/distinguishing-appearance-from-authentic-substance-reveals-hidden-conflicts.md) and see it's essence.

### Explanation

Similarly, intrinsic viewpoint often cultivates [Intrinsic motivation drives action through internal alignment and passion](/notes/intrinsic-motivation-drives-action-through-internal-alignment-and-passion.md), because our goal and the method are aligned, we don't study to get a reward, we study because we enjoy studying.

### Why it matters

There's a level of [Speaking truth about yourself and others enables authentic action](/notes/speaking-truth-about-yourself-and-others-enables-authentic-action.md) when we treat things based on their intrinsic rather than their instrumental values. We truly care about them, and won't discard them when a better alternative comes up. For example, managing a workplace through an intrinsic leans would lead to a [People are the most valuable organizational resource](/notes/people-are-the-most-valuable-organizational-resource.md) attitude, as they recognize the contribution and holistic nature of each person, rather than just as machines for a job.

### Examples

For example, a person who loves to play the guitar doesn't asks himself "what do I get out of it?", they are not focus on future earnings or social status, they play the guitar because that's what gives them enjoyment [Happiness is contentment](/notes/happiness-is-contentment.md). Intrinsic desires are when something is a goal of itself, and not a means to a different goal [Instrumental value treats things as means rather than ends](/notes/instrumental-value-treats-things-as-means-rather-than-ends.md).
