# Planning

## Notes

### Claim

Planning is [seeing](/notes/prediction.md) into the future and trying to actualize it in the present.

### Explanation

It could be considered as counter productive to action since we are using our time to think and plan instead of actually doing what we should, but planning is often a [Multiplier amplifies results through enhanced tools or behaviors](/notes/multiplier-amplifies-results-through-enhanced-tools-or-behaviors.md) to our productivity. By planning ahead, we can foresee potential risks [Pre-mortem anticipates failure modes to enable preparation](/notes/pre-mortem-anticipates-failure-modes-to-enable-preparation.md), we gain [Clarity is achieved through definition and presentation](/notes/clarity-is-achieved-through-definition-and-presentation.md) on when and how we are going to act, which increases our odds of actually doing so.

Planning is similar to [Goal Setting](/notes/clear-direction-matters-more-than-speed-of-progress.md), to try and bring clarity to the future by choosing our destination, and carving a path there.

### Why it matters

There's a saying "plans are worthless but planning is everything", meaning that since reality changes, the plan itself usually becomes irrelevant, especially as time goes by. However, the tools and mentality it promotes in the process is worthwhile.

A good plan can help us avoid distractions in advance by thinking [how](/notes/simulations-provide-safe-practice-environments-to-test-and-learn.md) to respond if something tries to derail us, although it's easier to plan for external things than internal ones.

### Examples

### Supporters

It can serve as a type of [One-time effort investment yields future benefits](/notes/one-time-effort-investment-yields-future-benefits.md), where we spend mental energy now, in order to free up resources when we start following it through.

### Opposers

However, planning is not bias proof. Often we [misjudge](/notes/we-underestimate-task-duration-through-overconfident-predictions.md) how long something would take and what will it cost, turning the plan into nothing more than an unrealistic dream.

Additionally, while it has advantages, it can become a form of [Procrastination is delaying intended actions despite preference to act](/notes/procrastination-is-delaying-intended-actions-despite-preference-to-act.md) if we use planning as an excuse for delaying our action, a form of [Perfectionism paralyzes action through unattainable standards](/notes/perfectionism-paralyzes-action-through-unattainable-standards.md), that we want to make sure "it's perfect" before we start, or to plan for every possible scenario.
