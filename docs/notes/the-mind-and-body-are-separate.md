# Mind-Body Duality

## Notes


### Claim

There is a separation between your body and your soul/mind.

### Explanation

According to this view, it is logical to assume that experiences in your body are external to "who you are", they are not indicative of your identity. For example - emotions, urges and hormones are considered as external forces trying to influence your consciousness [Achieving internal harmony requires integrating all parts of yourself](/notes/achieving-internal-harmony-requires-integrating-all-parts-of-yourself.md).

If this is true, than morality is only the result of logical inference [Truth is discoverable through reason and logic alone](/notes/truth-is-discoverable-through-reason-and-logic-alone.md), and doesn't involve any other type of "signals" such as [Some knowledge requires direct experience to fully understand](/notes/some-knowledge-requires-direct-experience-to-fully-understand.md). Meaning that it goes against the idea that [Morality is both rational and emotional](/notes/morality-is-both-rational-and-emotional.md)
