# No Pain no Gain

## Notes


### Claim

Sometimes we see [effort](/notes/struggle-is-an-inevitable-and-necessary-part-of-any-meaningful-growth-or-achievement.md) as a virtue, that achievements in life or having good things necessarily involves effort, so we assume that if we are not working hard, we won't achieve anything. If something is [simple](/notes/distilling-to-essentials-creates-clarity-and-actionability.md), or god forbid [fun](/notes/play.md), then it won't lead us anywhere, aka - "a smooth sea never made a skilled sailor".

### Explanation

### Why it matters

If I could have the same level of outputs with less inputs, less struggle and energy, why shouldn't I? Taking "shortcuts" or doing things "smarter" is not a vice, it is exactly the thing that will help us achieve what we wanted, and bypass competitors. [Flipping assumptions reveals hidden possibilities and better solutions](/notes/flipping-assumptions-reveals-hidden-possibilities-and-better-solutions.md)

### Opposers

The problem is that it often leads us to [Goodhart's Law](/notes/goodharts-law.md), where we focus on the effort as the metric for success, instead of our progress/outcome. We suffer for the sake of suffering, regardless of the output. We think that by suffering more, we will achieve more success, as if it is a direct link.
