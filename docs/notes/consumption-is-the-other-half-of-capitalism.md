# Consumerism

## Notes


### Claim

To keep the economic machine rolling, we need to have both aspects - production and consumption. [Capitalism](/notes/capitalism.md) "takes care" of the production side, while consumerism takes care of the consumption side. If one leg falls, they both fall.

### Explanation

Consumerism means that we focus on the [lack](/notes/scarcity-forces-prioritization-when-resources-are-limited.md) in our lives, and how [new things](/notes/distraction-by-novelty-undermines-commitment-and-depth.md) could potentially fill that gap. However, this is not a one-time solution. If we were to make a single purchase and be satisfied for life, the economy would crumble. It requires constant consumption. That's why consumerism leads to the psychological phenomena:
1. **Constant hunger for more** - What we have is never enough, we can never be satisfied with what we have, and the only way to solve our problems is to have more things [Deferring happiness to future goals creates present suffering](/notes/deferring-happiness-to-future-goals-creates-present-suffering.md). Since new inventions keep on coming and products wear and tear over time, we constantly have to replace them [Happiness is contentment](/notes/happiness-is-contentment.md). We become machines of [Desire perpetuates endless wanting over satisfaction](/notes/desire-perpetuates-endless-wanting-over-satisfaction.md).
2. **Happiness is having** - We no longer determine what makes us happy. The [economy](/notes/social-environment-shapes-behavior-and-identity-through-norms-and-conformity.md) bombards us with messages that happiness is a matter of possession, and no other forms are acceptable. We fall pry to [Shallow copies mistake partial representations for complete truth](/notes/shallow-copies-mistake-partial-representations-for-complete-truth.md) of happiness.
