# Complexity

## Notes

### Claim

Complexity is when an idea is hard to understand, or to implement [Distilling to essentials creates clarity and actionability](/notes/distilling-to-essentials-creates-clarity-and-actionability.md).

### Explanation

Complexity is not necessarily a bad thing because complex systems can be highly beneficials because they allow the combination between multiple viewpoints [Synergy](/notes/win-win-outcomes-align-interests-and-benefit-all-parties.md), taking into account many variables and giving a nuanced result [Diversity of perspectives strengthens decision-making and understanding](/notes/diversity-of-perspectives-strengthens-decision-making-and-understanding.md).

A common issue with complexity is the increased complexity in human life. [Innovation is generating something genuinely new that hasn't existed before](/notes/innovation-is-generating-something-genuinely-new-that-hasnt-existed-before.md), education and productivity require us to constantly improve, making it harder and harder as time goes by because of [Each additional unit provides progressively less value](/notes/each-additional-unit-provides-progressively-less-value.md).
Sometimes it leads to the [Sometimes you must keep adapting just to stay in place](/notes/sometimes-you-must-keep-adapting-just-to-stay-in-place.md), that you need to run nonstop just to stay in the same place.

### Why it matters

Complexity drives us towards digital and shared solutions, both stem from the same assumption that today's lives are too complex to be done by one person and one [mind](/notes/thinking-extends-beyond-the-brain-to-environment-and-society.md). We can reduce complexity using todolists, checklists, habits and digital notetaking as a form of [One-time effort investment yields future benefits](/notes/one-time-effort-investment-yields-future-benefits.md), using these tools to think across time without rethinking each time from scratch.

Similarly, we can use others to solve shared problems [Peer support enables collaborative growth through shared feedback and mutual learning](/notes/peer-support-enables-collaborative-growth-through-shared-feedback-and-mutual-learning.md). We need diversity of skills in order to meet the needs of advanced culture and to solve difficult [Problems](/notes/problems.md)

### Examples

If 1000 years ago, just be being literate you could have been the most successful merchant, 100 years ago you needed to have basic math, and today you need computer skills, and soft skills, etc.

Our economy is based on an assumption that each person focuses on what they do best [Finding your unique strength creates sustainable advantage](/notes/finding-your-unique-strength-creates-sustainable-advantage.md), because a single person can't be their own farmer, clothing maker, mechanic and driver.

### Supporters

Since [Our mind is a processor and not a warehouse](/notes/our-mind-is-a-processor-and-not-a-warehouse.md), we can reduce complexity using external tools to offload cognitive burden.
