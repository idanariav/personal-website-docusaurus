# Feynman Technique

## Notes


### Claim

the best way to learn is to teach [Active engagement with material accelerates learning](/notes/active-engagement-with-material-accelerates-learning.md).

### Explanation

the technique has 3 stages:

1. **Learn** - implement your favorite learning technique, could be using video, personal project, taking notes.
2. **Present** - Make a video/article/presentation for someone who has no knowledge in this topic. "If you can't explain it to a six year old, you don't understand it yourself." - Albert Einstein. [Distilling to essentials creates clarity and actionability](/notes/distilling-to-essentials-creates-clarity-and-actionability.md)
3. **Critique** - reread/watch what you made, look for 3 things:
	- Factual mistakes - things you said incorrectly
	- Unclear material - something that would have confused the listener, that they wouldn't understand
	- Follow-up questions: what does that make you think about? what might listeners ask about?

### Why it matters

This helps us see the world through the eyes of a [beginner](/notes/openness-to-new-ideas-without-expert-bias.md), to think about what they know, and how to transfer the knowledge to them.

Aside from having better knowledge in the specific area we are trying to teach, it turns us into better learners in general, and improves our skill at learning. We are more aware of the importance of [Mental shortcuts enable faster learning through analogies and simplification](/notes/mental-shortcuts-enable-faster-learning-through-analogies-and-simplification.md) and how to recreate them for people who think differently.

### Supporters

The beauty and simplicity of this technique has many layers. First of all, when we are [forced](/notes/we-understand-less-than-we-think.md) to try and implement what we study, and pass it on to others, we are more [enaged](/notes/active-engagement-turns-information-into-knowledge.md) with the material, so we learn better. We know that now the stakes are much higher. To learn incorrectly can result in an [embarassing](/notes/guilt-signals-when-we-fail-to-act-on-what-we-control.md) experience when reality would hit us in the face when we will see how little we know. It is one of the best types of [Feedback is information that enables behavioral improvement](/notes/feedback-is-information-that-enables-behavioral-improvement.md) we can get.

It also encourages us to [distill](/notes/distilling-separates-essence-from-noise-to-simplify-understanding.md) large quantities of knowledge into a simple and clear message. It is not easy to do because [We can't read minds](/notes/understanding-others-requires-attributing-mental-states-like-beliefs-and-intentions-to-them.md), but it does improve our [Mindsight is awareness of mental states in self and others](/notes/mindsight-is-awareness-of-mental-states-in-self-and-others.md) capabilities. We see the gaps in our knowledge, and we understand others better.

This technique is perhaps the hardest of them all, but [Learning should be hard](/notes/learning-should-be-hard.md).

### Opposers

Additionally, it encourages us to step beyond our own [Perspective](/notes/subjective-perception-filters-how-we-see-external-reality.md).
