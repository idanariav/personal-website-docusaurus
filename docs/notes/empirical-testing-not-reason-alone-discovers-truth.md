# Scientific Method

## Notes

### Claim

The scientific method is a way of thinking about the world and what we [know](/notes/truth.md) about it. The core idea is that in order to increase our knowledge and our confidence in it, the only viable method is by [Experimentation](/notes/experimentation-is-the-only-reliable-way-to-improve-and-discover-truth.md), instead of relying solely on our prior knowledge, reasoning [Truth is discoverable through reason and logic alone](/notes/truth-is-discoverable-through-reason-and-logic-alone.md), and [Gut Feeling](/notes/heuristics-are-mental-shortcuts-enabling-fast-automatic-decisions.md).

### Explanation

We gain knowledge by [Hypothesis Testing](/notes/hypothesis-testing.md). An hypothesis is an idea that can be rejected under verifiable conditions. For example, an hypothesis can be "all swans are black". By finding a white swan, our hypothesis will be rejected and we will update our knowledge accordingly.

This method on the one hand is much more humble and rigorous in terms of what we know (or assume to know). A scientist is much more open to [Rethinking means updating beliefs by reconsidering what you thought you knew](/notes/rethinking-means-updating-beliefs-by-reconsidering-what-you-thought-you-knew.md), to accept facts that contradict their beliefs and change accordingly [Conformation bias makes us accept confirming evidence without scrutiny](/notes/conformation-bias-makes-us-accept-confirming-evidence-without-scrutiny.md). At some level, to think like a scientist requires [Skepticism](/notes/examining-ideas-rigorously-before-accepting-them-as-true.md), that we don't accept any claim at face value; we first examine the claim and only believe it if it is true.

However, we can't rely on the scientific method alone. Even scientists can fall into biases that are typical to the scientific method, such as [Focusing on easy metrics blinds us to what matters](/notes/focusing-on-easy-metrics-blinds-us-to-what-matters.md), which means that we focus on what is easy to measure, quantify or to test, or that we fall to [Treating measurable metrics as complete truth hides importance](/notes/treating-measurable-metrics-as-complete-truth-hides-importance.md), when we claim that only what's measurable is important. Also, we might confuse [Confusing current reality with how things should be blocks change](/notes/confusing-current-reality-with-how-things-should-be-blocks-change.md), since causality is hard to measure; we might use what we see as a justification to what should be, instead of searching for the reasons for its existence.

### Why it Matters

The scientific method comes short when the truth we are trying to measure and gain is not [objective](/notes/seeing-reality-without-subjective-bias-reduces-distortion.md), when there is no one true value, rather many options that are all true at the same time, or at least somewhat overlap. For example, human behavior is often such a case, when there is no clear answer for many of life's questions.
