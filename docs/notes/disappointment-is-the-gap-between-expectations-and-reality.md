# Disappointment

## Notes

### Claim

Disappointment is when our [Expectations shape wellbeing through the gap between desires and reality](/notes/expectations-shape-wellbeing-through-the-gap-between-desires-and-reality.md) don't meet reality. The more we expect things of life, of others, especially things that are outside of our control [Accepting what we cannot control is the path to peace](/notes/accepting-what-we-cannot-control-is-the-path-to-peace.md), the more we are likely to feel disappointment [Life is pain](/notes/life-is-pain.md).

### Explanation

### Why it matters

Disappointment is the opposite of [Focus on what you can do next not what went wrong](/notes/focus-on-what-you-can-do-next-not-what-went-wrong.md), instead of looking ahead and thinking what we could do differently now, we focus on the past and how awful the current situation is.

### Examples

### Supporters

Disappointment might also lead to [We regret inaction more than action taken](/notes/we-regret-inaction-more-than-action-taken.md), thinking that in hindsight we should have acted differently.
