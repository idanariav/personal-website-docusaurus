# Priming

## Notes

### Claim

Priming is when we receive a stimulus from the environment which promotes a certain behavior.

### Explanation

While it can involve [How we describe something shapes how it is perceived](/notes/how-we-describe-something-shapes-how-it-is-perceived.md), a cue can be something more direct and pure, affecting our [Automatic thinking processes drive fast emotional responses](/notes/automatic-thinking-processes-drive-fast-emotional-responses.md) thinking, like a smell, a sight, or even the presence of someone. A cue is a form of [Emotional hijacking is uncontrolled System 1 dominance over reasoning](/notes/emotional-hijacking-is-uncontrolled-system-1-dominance-over-reasoning.md), when the situation triggers emotions that [propel](/notes/impulsivity-is-acting-without-conscious-deliberation-or-filter.md) us for [action](/notes/emotions-provide-decision-relevant-information-when-navigating-choices.md).

It can also involve more complex forms of triggers, such as our [Social environment shapes behavior and identity through norms and conformity](/notes/social-environment-shapes-behavior-and-identity-through-norms-and-conformity.md), the norms, cultures and behaviors that shape our [Perspective](/notes/subjective-perception-filters-how-we-see-external-reality.md).

### Why it Matters

A cue isn't good or bad, the behavior it generates is what's important. Even though a cue pushes towards certain behavior, and with time this behavior can become a [habit](/notes/repeated-actions-become-automatic-through-habit-formation.md), we can design our environment intentionally.

With a beneficial [Environmental design](/notes/environmental-design-influences-behavior-through-choice-architecture.md), you can create an environment with only the cues you want to have, those who promote the behavior you are seeking. Essentially, the cues we revolve ourselves with create a form of [Past choices constrain future options and behavior](/notes/past-choices-constrain-future-options-and-behavior.md), where certain behaviors are much more likely than others.

### Examples

A classic example is a hunger for junk food when seeing snacks, being angry or scared after seeing a commercial, and feeling bad when we smell something awful.

We tend to feel more competitive and less cooperative at work. We behave differently in different social groups [Identity is shaped by and shapes our social interactions](/notes/identity-is-shaped-by-and-shapes-our-social-interactions.md), and we act differently when we feel being watched or reminded of our values (like after taking an oath or seeing the ten commandments).
