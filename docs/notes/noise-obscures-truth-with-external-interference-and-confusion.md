# Noise

## Notes


### Claim

Noise is all the external signals that are leading us [away](/notes/one-activity-displaces-another-when-pursued-excessively.md) from the [Truth](/notes/truth.md) and confuses us.

### Explanation

This can be statistically by expressing as an error that disrupts the trends and can cause errors of [fake correlations](/notes/correlation-is-not-causation.md). But also in the personal sense where we are bombarded by information [Our attention is the product that media companies sell](/notes/our-attention-is-the-product-that-media-companies-sell.md), and we have to learn to [filter](/notes/filtering-is-choosing-what-to-consume-before-distilling-it.md) unhelpful, damaging, and simply wrong messages out of the quality ones.

### Why it matters

It is important to remember that when it comes to noises, the [Most benefits come from a small proportion of inputs](/notes/most-benefits-come-from-a-small-proportion-of-inputs.md) usually applies, meaning that most of the noise is junk, and a small part of it is helpful. Also it is worth [distilling](/notes/distilling-separates-essence-from-noise-to-simplify-understanding.md) it and actively filter it as to not let yourself be affected by the misleading part, to extract the good from the bland. Perhaps if we [align](/notes/resonance-is-being-in-sync-with-reality-and-others.md) ourselves with what we try to learn, a connection to the truth, to the basics, could be helpful with that.
