# Competitive Advantage

## Notes


### Claim

Competitive Advantage is finding the thing you are best at [Mastery requires deliberate practice and hard work, not innate talent](/notes/mastery-requires-deliberate-practice-and-hard-work-not-innate-talent.md). That doesn't mean you have to be the best in the world in, but rather this is the most effective outcome you can make.

### Explanation

In real life, it is tough to know what you competitive advantage is, it requires [Self-awareness reveals mental and emotional states enabling intentional action](/notes/self-awareness-reveals-mental-and-emotional-states-enabling-intentional-action.md) of your skills and capabilities, and also to measure the quality of your work, which can be difficult due to both [Overestimating abilities blinds us to genuine risks](/notes/overestimating-abilities-blinds-us-to-genuine-risks.md) and [Imposter syndrome is feeling undeserving despite actual competence](/notes/imposter-syndrome-is-feeling-undeserving-despite-actual-competence.md). Similarly, since we are focusing on what counts as an advantage compared to others, we can't escape making [Comparing ourselves to others damages wellbeing and progress](/notes/comparing-ourselves-to-others-damages-wellbeing-and-progress.md), although we should focus on using these to gain information what to do next, rather than to discourage our progress.

Often, your competitive advantage is the result of your [Unique skill combinations create competitive and creative advantage](/notes/unique-skill-combinations-create-competitive-and-creative-advantage.md), the unique combination of skills and perspective that only you (and perhaps few others) can provide. A competitive advantage can either be a result of a [Specialization develops deep expertise by focusing narrowly on a domain](/notes/specialization-develops-deep-expertise-by-focusing-narrowly-on-a-domain.md) in a specific field, or pursuing a [Range](/notes/breath-beats-depth.md) of different fields and combine their domain knowledge together.

### Why it matters

Usually, a competitive advantage doesn't last forever, we have to keep honing it. To continue to [optimize](/notes/optimization-finds-the-efficient-point-balancing-outputs-and-inputs.md) and [practice](/notes/deliberate-practice-is-the-best-way-to-develop-skills.md) it constantly to maintain our advantage. If we stay in place, we would fall behind.

### Examples

If someone is either able to produce 5 oranges or 5 apples, and you are capable of producing 2 oranges or 1 apple, then clearly you should stick with oranges, even though someone else is still better than you in both categories.
