# A Person is a Community

## Notes

### Claim
We are never just a single entity, rather a combination of multiple identities.

### Explanation
Our multitude can be roles we have in life: parent, teacher, friend, i.e different parts of ourselves that we embody for different people [Identity is shaped by and shapes our social interactions](/notes/identity-is-shaped-by-and-shapes-our-social-interactions.md). It can be our inspirations: spiritually, professionally, and it can also be our desires, emotional, present vs future [Identity persists across time through psychological continuity](/notes/identity-persists-across-time-through-psychological-continuity.md), almost any aspect of human life can be something that we are divided on. [Diversity of perspectives strengthens decision-making and understanding](/notes/diversity-of-perspectives-strengthens-decision-making-and-understanding.md) is built into us.

### Why it matters
As in any community, in order to avoid these conflicts, we need to find balance by converging these entities together into a unified voice [Achieving internal harmony requires integrating all parts of yourself](/notes/achieving-internal-harmony-requires-integrating-all-parts-of-yourself.md).
The process of unification or rather [Deliberation is a discussion that bridges different perspectives](/notes/deliberation-is-a-discussion-that-bridges-different-perspectives.md) is not easy, but so is living with conflicts. Neglecting can be harmful to our well-being.

### Supporters
It requires [Being kind to yourself strengthens resilience and growth](/notes/being-kind-to-yourself-strengthens-resilience-and-growth.md), so that we could [listen](/notes/listening-well-requires-active-effort-to-understand.md) and [accept](/notes/accepting-others-feelings-and-perspectives-without-dismissal.md) even the "negative" voices with us, and [Introspection develops self-knowledge through internal examination](/notes/introspection-develops-self-knowledge-through-internal-examination.md) in order to realize where are the sources of each of these voices, why do they exist, and what they are trying to accomplish.

In some sense, this also means that [What we do is not who we are](/notes/separating-actions-from-identity-allows-growth.md), because we can only do one thing at a time, while effectively we can have different voices competing inside of us constantly.
