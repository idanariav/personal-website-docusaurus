# Pivot

## Notes

### Claim

To pivot means to make a small experiment to test out your assumptions.

### Explanation

When we [Starting small reduces resistance and builds momentum for change](/notes/starting-small-reduces-resistance-and-builds-momentum-for-change.md) , we can reduce risk (aka:: [We prefer avoiding losses over gaining equivalent value](/notes/we-prefer-avoiding-losses-over-gaining-equivalent-value.md) ).

### Why it matters

Test the water before diving straight in.

Pivoting can also allow you to [Generating multiple alternatives simultaneously improves outcomes](/notes/generating-multiple-alternatives-simultaneously-improves-outcomes.md), to work on several topics simultaniously. Sometimes, although not always, if those things are synergetic, pivoting can help you develop your [Unique skill combinations create competitive and creative advantage](/notes/unique-skill-combinations-create-competitive-and-creative-advantage.md) that would not have been possible if you maintained only one profession.

### Examples

For example, instead of quitting your job to start a whole new career as a freelancer, try to do one small project as a side hustle, or before choosing a degree and committing for several years, shadow a graduate from that field for a day and see what they do.
