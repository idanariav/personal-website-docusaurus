# Change Starts from the inside out

## Notes

### Claim

When you try to change yourself, don't start by focusing on your actions—like saying "I want to lose 5 pounds"—but rather focus on the identity you want to adapt: "I want to be a healthy person".

### Explanation

Focusing on the identity provides the [motivation](/notes/intrinsic-motivation-drives-action-through-internal-alignment-and-passion.md), the direction, and the longevity of a successful change, instead of actions which are short-sighted, lack the "why", and irrelevant once completed [Focusing on process and consistency yields better results than obsessing over outcomes](/notes/focusing-on-process-and-consistency-yields-better-results-than-obsessing-over-outcomes.md). Instead of forcing ourselves to act in a certain way, we rewrite our [life narrative](/notes/narratives-shape-perception-and-identity-by-constructing-subjective-reality.md) in a way that makes us [wish](/notes/love-what-is-good.md) to embrace that new lifestyle.

When we embrace a new identity, we allow ourselves to change across time [Identity persists across time through psychological continuity](/notes/identity-persists-across-time-through-psychological-continuity.md). We manage to implement our [Examine your desires not just your actions](/notes/examine-your-desires-not-just-your-actions.md) as a source for change. This is opposite to the idea of [Being emerges from action not belief](/notes/being-emerges-from-action-not-belief.md), the idea that change, learning, and being comes from experimentation and action, not self affirming statements.

However, embracing a new identity is not so easy. One can argue that even in the best case scenario, our identities are not replaced, rather [layered](/notes/learning-adds-new-knowledge-layers-instead-of-replacing-old-understanding.md) on top of another, so the range of potential identities that we can embrace is limited by who we were in the past.

### Why it matters

When we embrace a new identity rather than just changing our actions, we implement a [Capabilities are malleable through effort and learning](/notes/capabilities-are-malleable-through-effort-and-learning.md), we prove that not only we can act differently, we can become a different, better person. Also, claiming a new identity is like a [Fresh start](/notes/symbolic-fresh-starts-break-entrenchment-and-enable-change.md)—we are not limited by the actions, tendencies and behaviors of our "past self". Rather we are now someone new who could adopt a new lifestyle without previous baggage.
