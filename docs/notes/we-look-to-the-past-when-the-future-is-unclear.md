# Nostalgia

## Notes


### Claim

**If we can't look to others or to the future, then we can only look at the past and inwards**.

### Explanation

Since [Postmodernism rejects universal truth in favor of context-dependent meaning](/notes/postmodernism-rejects-universal-truth-in-favor-of-context-dependent-meaning.md) is about [deconstruction](/notes/deconstruction.md), we can no longer have unified [Narratives shape perception and identity by constructing subjective reality](/notes/narratives-shape-perception-and-identity-by-constructing-subjective-reality.md) or grand ideologies that combine us, or give us purpose and goals for the future, after all everything is either [relative](/notes/what-is-moral-depends-on-the-viewer.md) or without meaning. **We miss those times where we were unified, where we felt part of something bigger**.

### Why it matters

Now we are highly individualized, replacing grand dreams with quantitative "cold" measures of success like money or status. [Image vs core](/notes/distinguishing-appearance-from-authentic-substance-reveals-hidden-conflicts.md) [Commodification turns non-tradable values into economic transactions](/notes/commodification-turns-non-tradable-values-into-economic-transactions.md)

### Examples

We become [narcistics](/notes/preferring-personal-benefits-blinds-us-to-others-perspectives.md) and nostalgic, no wonder our economy is selling us nostalgia, and focuses more on [remixing](/notes/imitation-internalizes-others-behaviors-and-mental-models-at-varying-depths.md) than creating something new.
