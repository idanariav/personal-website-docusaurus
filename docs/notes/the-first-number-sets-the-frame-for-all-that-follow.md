# Anchoring

## Notes


### Claim

Anchoring is a method by which we reduce [Ambiguity is the absence of clarity in values or facts](/notes/ambiguity-is-the-absence-of-clarity-in-values-or-facts.md) by setting a concrete value for comparison in a given topic. Since it makes it easier for comparison, by the way of least resistance we are "forced" to use this value as our frame of reference even if it is far from the actual value.

### Explanation

### Why it matters

Therefore, anchoring is way to affect the [Subjective perception filters how we see external reality](/notes/subjective-perception-filters-how-we-see-external-reality.md) in others.

### Examples

For example, asking you whether Uganda has a population of higher or lower than 1 million would make you give a lower estimate in comparison with if I had asked about higher or lower than 10 million.
