# Habit Formation

## Notes


### Claim

Habit formation is how certain actions are converted from energy intensive actions to ones that are done automatically.

### Explanation

The reason our brain converts things into habits is to save energy. It "knows" that if we are [repeating](/notes/repeated-exposure-to-information-strengthens-memory-retention.md) an action it must be useful or important, so why not make it easier to do? [We optimize by keeping the most-used close at hand](/notes/we-optimize-by-keeping-the-most-used-close-at-hand.md). It turns from a [concious](/notes/full-working-memory-reduces-focus-and-task-performance.md) thought into an automatic process, moving it from [System 2 enables deliberate analytical thinking for complex problems](/notes/system-2-enables-deliberate-analytical-thinking-for-complex-problems.md) to [Automatic thinking processes drive fast emotional responses](/notes/automatic-thinking-processes-drive-fast-emotional-responses.md) and constantly reinforced through [Habits operate through a four-part loop](/notes/habits-operate-through-a-four-part-loop.md).

Habits will probably not form for actions that are difficult, i.e involves much [Friction is resistance that blocks action and behavior adoption](/notes/friction-is-resistance-that-blocks-action-and-behavior-adoption.md) or require [Examining ideas rigorously before accepting them as true](/notes/examining-ideas-rigorously-before-accepting-them-as-true.md) of any sort.

Similarly, since habits are all about repeatability, they are easier to form for [small](/notes/starting-small-reduces-resistance-and-builds-momentum-for-change.md), [fun](/notes/gamification-increases-motivation-by-making-activities-enjoyable.md) activities.

Habits usually require a [Cue](/notes/environmental-stimuli-trigger-automatic-behavioral-responses.md), a signal for the brain to start the automated action. If we use [Environmental design](/notes/environmental-design-influences-behavior-through-choice-architecture.md) wisely, we can choose which cues exist in our environment so that we could enable/prevent certain habits from occurring.

### Why it matters

Habits are a good way for creating [routines](/notes/focusing-on-process-and-consistency-yields-better-results-than-obsessing-over-outcomes.md) for a better lifestyle, instead of relying on our [limited willpower](/notes/willpower-is-limited.md)
