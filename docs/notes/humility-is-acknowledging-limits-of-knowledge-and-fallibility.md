# Humility

## Notes

### Claim

Humility is perhaps the opposite of [Overestimating abilities blinds us to genuine risks](/notes/overestimating-abilities-blinds-us-to-genuine-risks.md), it is to put our [Ego](/notes/distinguishing-appearance-from-authentic-substance-reveals-hidden-conflicts.md) aside, and acknowledge that we might make mistakes and don't know everything, and to be satisfied with who we are and what we have [Minimalism](/notes/improvement-often-comes-from-removing-rather-than-adding.md). You don't need [Self-awareness reveals mental and emotional states enabling intentional action](/notes/self-awareness-reveals-mental-and-emotional-states-enabling-intentional-action.md) to be humble, you might be humble as a "precaution", but it often goes together.

### Explanation

Humility is very difficult to [balance](/notes/optimal-outcomes-emerge-from-balance-between-extremes-not-absolutes.md), if we have too little, we might fall back into over confidence, if we have too much, we might experience [Imposter syndrome is feeling undeserving despite actual competence](/notes/imposter-syndrome-is-feeling-undeserving-despite-actual-competence.md). It requires to have sufficient [Confidence](/notes/self-worth-is-accurate-assessment-of-your-capabilities.md), yet not too much of it.

### Why it matters

Practicing humility can help us stay in a [Openness to new ideas without expert bias](/notes/openness-to-new-ideas-without-expert-bias.md), to be "forever the student" in new situations, recognizing that we always have much to [learn](/notes/capabilities-are-malleable-through-effort-and-learning.md), to be [curious](/notes/curiosity-is-the-joy-of-filling-knowledge-gaps.md), and to be open to other viewpoints. Humility also goes well with the [Empirical testing not reason alone discovers truth](/notes/empirical-testing-not-reason-alone-discovers-truth.md). We don't get too [attached](/notes/attachment-distorts-how-we-see-the-things-we-cling-to.md) to whatever we believe, knowing that we might be wrong, and we can easily [align](/notes/our-beliefs-must-adapt-as-context-and-goals-change.md) ourselves based on the evidence.
