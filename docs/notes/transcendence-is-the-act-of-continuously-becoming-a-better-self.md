# Transcendence

## Notes

### Claim

Transcendence is an act of becoming, of developing ourselves towards a better "version" of ourselves, of becoming an [idea](/notes/subjectivity.md), to aspire to be more, to do more.

### Explanation

Transcendence is the fulfillment and creation of our potential, it is to [live](/notes/acting-in-alignment-with-your-values-is-authenticity.md) according to our [values](/notes/core-values-guide-authentic-identity-and-moral-action.md) and practice virtues. It's about the journey, not the destination.

Transcendence is a [process](/notes/focusing-on-process-and-consistency-yields-better-results-than-obsessing-over-outcomes.md), not a goal. We treat ourselves as a [never ending](/notes/to-be-is-to-be-incomplete.md) project that on the one hand needs constant refining, but on the other hand brings satisfaction.

### Why it matters

Transcendence requires many attributes in order for us not to fall off the path.

### Examples

We can develop transcendence by:

1. **Taking control** - [Grit enables persisting through struggle and challenge](/notes/grit-enables-persisting-through-struggle-and-challenge.md) gives us the determination to continue developing ourselves despite hardships. We don't believe that we are [fixed](/notes/fixed-mindset-assumes-capabilities-are-unchangeable-from-birth.md); we can change, we can grow, we can improve, and we have the [power](/notes/the-will-to-power-is-the-inner-drive-to-transcend-oneself-and-become-something-greater.md) to [choose](/notes/agency-is-the-ability-to-connect-desires-and-actions.md) who we would become. We will [change](/notes/identity-persists-across-time-through-psychological-continuity.md) over time, and we control the direction we are heading.

2. **Self knowledge** - We must know ourselves, our thoughts and feelings; otherwise we have no idea where we are, so how could we know where we want to get to and how to get there? [Self-awareness reveals mental and emotional states enabling intentional action](/notes/self-awareness-reveals-mental-and-emotional-states-enabling-intentional-action.md)

3. **Self compassion** - Despite the desire to change, if we do it in a [confrontational](/notes/self-criticism-is-being-your-own-harsh-judge.md) way, if we treat ourselves as something worthless to be discarded and replaced with the new, we would only cause internal conflicts and become a shattered human being. We need a delicate balance between [loving](/notes/being-kind-to-yourself-strengthens-resilience-and-growth.md) who we are right now, while wishing to become a "better you". We don't replace who we are; we [refine](/notes/learning-adds-new-knowledge-layers-instead-of-replacing-old-understanding.md) it. We use our [Achieving internal harmony requires integrating all parts of yourself](/notes/achieving-internal-harmony-requires-integrating-all-parts-of-yourself.md) as a source of strength, not limitation. It is through the act of self acceptance that we can become better people.
