# Analysis Paralysis

## Notes


### Claim

Analysis paralysis is when we are overwhelmed with information and can't make up our mind [Facing unlimited options paralyzes decision and breeds helplessness](/notes/facing-unlimited-options-paralyzes-decision-and-breeds-helplessness.md). The situation is too [complex](/notes/complexity.md) for us to handle, too many things to consider at once, and we can't [distill](/notes/distilling-separates-essence-from-noise-to-simplify-understanding.md) them into a single decision rule.

### Explanation

Either we fail to decide because holding all the information in our mind is [too much](/notes/taking-on-too-much-overwhelms-capacity-and-causes-depletion.md) for us to handle [our brain is meant for processing](/notes/our-mind-is-a-processor-and-not-a-warehouse.md), or perhaps some decisions can't be resolved using rationality, and require a [Acting before understanding requires trusting instinct and resonance](/notes/acting-before-understanding-requires-trusting-instinct-and-resonance.md)
