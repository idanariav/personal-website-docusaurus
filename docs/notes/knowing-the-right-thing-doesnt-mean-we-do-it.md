# Akrasia

## Notes


### Claim

When someone acts against his better judgement, i.e lack [Self-control is the capacity to regulate behavior against impulses](/notes/self-control-is-the-capacity-to-regulate-behavior-against-impulses.md). It is also often called the Insight Gap, when we know what is true/what we need to do, but fail to do so.

### Explanation

Knowing is not [enough](/notes/free-will-is-knowledge.md), there is something that holds us back even though we want do [Examine your desires not just your actions](/notes/examine-your-desires-not-just-your-actions.md). This perhaps demonstrate the difference between making a decisions in a real life, vs philosophical experiments where we debate what we would have done behind the [Design society as if you don't know your place in it](/notes/design-society-as-if-you-dont-know-your-place-in-it.md).

This suggests that our behavior is more than just the result of our logical thinking. Considering the power of [Emotional hijacking is uncontrolled System 1 dominance over reasoning](/notes/emotional-hijacking-is-uncontrolled-system-1-dominance-over-reasoning.md), there are many cases where we can end up doing things we eventually [We regret inaction more than action taken](/notes/we-regret-inaction-more-than-action-taken.md). This can be the result of [We prefer immediate benefits over delayed ones](/notes/we-prefer-immediate-benefits-over-delayed-ones.md), when we "surrender" to our short term [Temptations](/notes/distraction.md) that are hurtful in the long run, and fail to provide meaningful happiness.

That suggests that at least on some level we have an [intrapersonal conflict](/notes/a-person-is-a-community.md), that two or more voices within ourselves are not in agreement.
